You know why creating front-end is more challenging with AI than backend or non-UI code?  

I think it's because we lack a way to communicate the design in text, like how a designer sits and visualizes how it looks, experiments, and makes adjustments.

A designer doesn't tell the tool she is using, "Hey, move this button three pixels." Instead, she just makes mouse clicks, drags, and drops, or follows guides from design system frameworks.   

One key step to improve your AI's design capabilities is: 

\-Create a design framework with a guide and reusable components. 

\-Ask AI to use it, as you instruct with your specs.

\-Spend the “tedious” time to design those documents and components carefully. 

We had so many competing design frameworks in one of the companies I worked for; if you can stick with one of them, they are really useful and required to make the UI consistent. 

That helps for AI as well. 

Just please do not promote people because they created a new design framework, because the previous one was not "cool".:)
